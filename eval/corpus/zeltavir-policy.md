# Zeltavir (zeltavirmab) — Prior Authorization Policy

Policy number: PA-2291
Effective date: 1 March 2025
Last revised: 15 January 2025
Formulary tier: Tier 4 (specialty)
Review cycle: annual

## Purpose

This policy establishes the medical necessity criteria for Zeltavir, a humanised monoclonal
antibody indicated for moderate to severe rheumatoid arthritis. It applies to all members
enrolled in SilverCare PPO and GoldCare HMO.

## Coverage criteria

Zeltavir requires prior authorization before it will be covered under any plan. Authorization
is granted only when all of the following criteria are met:

1. The member is at least 18 years of age.
2. The member has a documented diagnosis of moderate to severe rheumatoid arthritis, confirmed
   by a rheumatologist.
3. The member has tried and failed at least one conventional DMARD, such as methotrexate, for a
   minimum of three consecutive months.
4. A supervised test dose has been administered in a clinical setting, and the member has been
   observed for no fewer than 60 minutes following administration without a hypersensitivity
   reaction.
5. The prescriber is a rheumatologist, or the prescription is written in consultation with one.

The test dose requirement is specific to Zeltavir and does not apply to any other biologic on
this formulary. It exists because of the observed rate of infusion reactions during the
pivotal trials.

## Exclusions

Zeltavir will not be authorised in any of the following circumstances:

- Concurrent use with another biologic DMARD or a JAK inhibitor.
- Active tuberculosis, or latent tuberculosis that has not completed treatment.
- Active serious infection requiring intravenous antimicrobial therapy.
- Pregnancy, unless the prescriber documents that benefit outweighs risk.

Concurrent use with Morvanex is specifically excluded. Requests listing both agents are denied
without clinical review.

## Authorization period

Once approved, authorization for Zeltavir remains valid for 12 months from the date of
approval. Reauthorization requires documented clinical benefit, defined as a reduction of at
least 20 percent in tender joint count from baseline, measured at or after week 12.

If reauthorization is not submitted before the authorization expires, the member must restart
the full initial review, including a repeat test dose.

## Diagnosis codes

The following ICD-10 codes are accepted for Zeltavir prior authorization requests:

- M05.79 — Rheumatoid arthritis with rheumatoid factor, multiple sites
- M06.09 — Rheumatoid arthritis without rheumatoid factor, multiple sites

Code M06.09 was added to this policy in the January 2025 revision. Requests submitted with any
other diagnosis code will be denied administratively rather than clinically, and may be
corrected and resubmitted without a formal appeal.

## Quantity limits

Zeltavir is limited to two 40 mg prefilled syringes per 28-day period. Quantity limit
exceptions require a separate review and are not granted at initial authorization. Exception
requests are decided within 15 business days.

The first fill is limited to a 14-day supply so that tolerability can be confirmed before a
full month is dispensed.

## Site of care

Initial administration must occur in an outpatient infusion centre or physician's office.
Self-administration at home is permitted only after the third successful dose, and only after
the member or a caregiver has completed the injection training module.

Home infusion is not covered for the first three doses under either plan.

## Specialty pharmacy requirement

Zeltavir must be dispensed through the designated specialty pharmacy network. Retail pharmacy
claims for Zeltavir will reject at point of sale with rejection code 70 (product not covered at
this location).

Members may contact the specialty pharmacy coordinator at 1-800-555-0177 to transfer a
prescription.

## Appeals

A denial of Zeltavir prior authorization may be appealed under the standard appeals process
described in document ADM-118. Because Zeltavir is a specialty agent, expedited review is
available where a treatment delay would jeopardise the member's ability to regain maximum
function.
