# Appeals and Grievance Procedures

Document reference: ADM-118
Effective date: 1 January 2025
Applies to all plans.

## Filing an appeal

A member or an authorised representative may appeal any adverse coverage determination,
including a denied prior authorization request. Appeals must be filed within 180 calendar days
of the date on the denial notice.

An appeal may be submitted in writing to the Appeals Unit, by secure message through the member
portal, or by telephone for expedited requests. A representative other than the treating
prescriber must supply a signed appointment-of-representative form before the appeal is opened.

## Standard appeals

Standard appeals are decided within 30 calendar days of receipt. If additional clinical
information is requested from the prescriber, the clock is paused for up to 14 calendar days
while that information is gathered.

Members receive written notice of the decision within five business days of the determination.
The notice states the specific policy criterion that was not met and the evidence reviewed.

## Expedited appeals

An expedited appeal is available when the standard 30-day timeframe could seriously jeopardise
the member's life, health, or ability to regain maximum function. Expedited appeals are decided
within 72 hours of receipt.

Requests for expedited review that do not meet the urgency standard are automatically
transferred to the standard appeals process, and the member is notified of the change by
telephone within one business day.

## Who reviews an appeal

Appeals of clinical denials are reviewed by a physician who was not involved in the original
determination and who practises in the same or a similar specialty as the treating prescriber.
For denials of Zeltavir and other specialty biologics, the reviewer is a board-certified
rheumatologist.

## Administrative denials

A request denied administratively — for example, one submitted with a diagnosis code not listed
in the applicable policy — may be corrected and resubmitted without filing a formal appeal.
Resubmission is not subject to the 180-day appeal window.

Administrative denials are distinguished from clinical denials on the denial notice by the
prefix ADM on the case number.

## External review

If an internal appeal is denied, the member may request an independent external review within
four months of the final internal denial. External review is conducted by an accredited
independent review organisation at no cost to the member.

External review decisions are binding on the plan. The plan must implement an overturned
determination within 72 hours of receiving the external reviewer's decision.

## Grievances

A grievance is distinct from an appeal. Grievances concern service quality — wait times, staff
conduct, facility access — rather than coverage determinations. Grievances are acknowledged
within five business days and resolved within 30 calendar days.

A grievance does not preserve appeal rights. A member dissatisfied with a coverage decision
must file an appeal within the 180-day window regardless of any grievance filed.

## Continuation of benefits

For an ongoing course of therapy already authorised, benefits continue during the appeal if the
member requests continuation before the effective date of the reduction or termination. If the
appeal is ultimately denied, the member may be responsible for the cost of the continued
therapy.

## Contacts

The Appeals Unit is reached at 1-800-555-0142. Expedited requests should state "expedited
review" at the start of the call. Written appeals should be addressed to the Appeals Unit,
PO Box 4120, and must include the case number from the denial notice.
