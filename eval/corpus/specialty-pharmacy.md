# Specialty Pharmacy and Claims Submission

Document reference: RX-540
Effective date: 1 January 2025

## Scope

This document describes how specialty medications are dispensed and how pharmacy claims are
submitted, adjudicated, and reversed. It applies to all Tier 4 medications, including Zeltavir.

## Designated specialty pharmacy network

All Tier 4 specialty medications must be dispensed through the designated specialty pharmacy
network. Retail pharmacy claims for a Tier 4 product reject at point of sale with rejection
code 70 (product not covered at this location).

The specialty network operates two dispensing centres and provides refrigerated overnight
shipping at no additional cost to the member. Delivery requires an adult signature.

The specialty pharmacy coordinator is reached at 1-800-555-0177.

## First-fill process

For a newly authorised specialty medication, the specialty pharmacy contacts the member within
two business days of receiving the approved authorization. The first fill is not shipped until
the member completes a welcome call covering storage, administration, and adverse-event
reporting.

For Zeltavir specifically, the first fill is limited to a 14-day supply so that tolerability can
be confirmed before a full month is dispensed. Subsequent fills follow the standard 28-day
quantity limit.

## Refill authorisation

Specialty refills are not dispensed automatically. The member must confirm each refill by
telephone or through the portal, no earlier than seven days before the supply runs out.

A refill requested more than seven days early rejects with code 79 (refill too soon). The
rejection can be overridden for documented travel, limited to twice per plan year.

## Claim rejection codes

| Code | Meaning | Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| 70 | Product not covered at this location | Transfer to specialty pharmacy |
| 75 | Prior authorization required | Prescriber submits PA request |
| 76 | Plan limitations exceeded | Request quantity limit exception |
| 79 | Refill too soon | Wait, or request a vacation override |
| 88 | DUR reject — drug interaction | Prescriber attests to clinical review |

Rejection code 75 is the most common rejection for Zeltavir at first fill, and usually means
the authorization has not yet been keyed into the claims system. Allow one business day after
approval before resubmitting.

## Claim reversals

A dispensed claim may be reversed within 14 calendar days of the fill date. Reversals after 14
days require a written request to the pharmacy help desk and are reviewed case by case.

A reversed claim restores the member's quantity limit accumulator, but does not restore the
authorization period, which continues to run from the original approval date.

## Copay assistance

Manufacturer copay assistance may be applied to Tier 3 and Tier 4 medications. Amounts paid by
a manufacturer assistance programme do not count toward the member's deductible or
out-of-pocket maximum.

Members should confirm the current accumulator balance with member services before assuming a
manufacturer payment has advanced them toward the out-of-pocket maximum.

## Coordination with prior authorization

This document governs dispensing only. Whether a specialty medication is authorised at all is
determined by the applicable drug policy — PA-2291 for Zeltavir and PA-2304 for Morvanex. A
claim cannot be adjudicated until the authorization is on file, regardless of which pharmacy
submits it.
