# Morvanex (morvanexib) — Prior Authorization Policy

Policy number: PA-2304
Effective date: 1 March 2025
Last revised: 2 February 2025
Formulary tier: Tier 3 (preferred brand)
Review cycle: annual

## Purpose

This policy establishes coverage criteria for Morvanex, an oral small-molecule therapy for
rheumatoid arthritis and primary generalized osteoarthritis. It applies to SilverCare PPO and
GoldCare HMO members.

## Coverage criteria

Morvanex does not require prior authorization for members enrolled in SilverCare PPO. For
GoldCare HMO members, prior authorization is required and is reviewed against the step therapy
criteria below.

No test dose is required for Morvanex under any plan. Members may begin therapy immediately
once coverage is confirmed. This is the principal operational difference between Morvanex and
Zeltavir, which does require a supervised test dose.

## Step therapy

GoldCare HMO members must have tried and failed two preferred alternatives before Morvanex is
approved:

1. A conventional DMARD for at least three consecutive months, and
2. One Tier 2 biosimilar for at least two consecutive months.

Step therapy may be bypassed when the prescriber documents a contraindication to both
alternatives, or documents that the member previously failed the alternative under a prior
carrier. Bypass requests are reviewed within five business days.

SilverCare PPO does not apply step therapy to Morvanex.

## Exclusions

Morvanex will not be authorised in any of the following circumstances:

- Concurrent use with a biologic DMARD, including Zeltavir.
- Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh class C).
- Absolute neutrophil count below 1,000 cells per microlitre at baseline.

## Authorization period

Where authorization applies, it remains valid for 6 months from the date of approval, after
which reauthorization is required. This is a shorter period than Zeltavir, which is authorised
for 12 months.

Reauthorization requires documentation of continued response and a current liver function
panel drawn within the preceding 60 days.

## Diagnosis codes

The following ICD-10 codes are accepted for Morvanex:

- M05.79 — Rheumatoid arthritis with rheumatoid factor, multiple sites
- M15.0 — Primary generalized osteoarthritis

Note that M06.09 is accepted for Zeltavir but is not accepted for Morvanex. A request for
Morvanex submitted under M06.09 will be denied administratively.

## Quantity limits

Morvanex is limited to one 20 mg tablet per day, dispensed in 30-day supplies. Mail-order
fulfilment is available for 90-day supplies at the standard tier copay.

Dose escalation to 40 mg daily requires a new prior authorization request and is limited to
members who have completed at least 12 weeks at the 20 mg dose.

## Monitoring requirements

Members on Morvanex require a complete blood count and liver function panel at baseline, at
week 8, and every six months thereafter. Failure to submit monitoring results at
reauthorization is grounds for denial of continued therapy.

## Site of care

Morvanex is an oral therapy and carries no site-of-care restriction. It may be filled at any
in-network retail pharmacy and does not require the specialty pharmacy network that applies to
Zeltavir.

## Appeals

Denials of Morvanex prior authorization follow the standard appeals process in document
ADM-118. Step therapy denials are among the most frequently overturned on appeal when the
prescriber supplies prior-carrier records.
